Description

The Coriell Cell Line Deletion and Duplication track shows genotyping of chromosomal aberration and inherited disorder samples from an NIGMS study (http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/projects/gap/cgi-bin/study.cgi?study_id=phs000269.v1.p1 , dbGaP Study Accession: phs000269.v1.p1).

716 samples derived from 697 individuals from the Chromosomal Aberrations and Inherited Disorders collections of the NIGMS Human Genetic Cell Repository were genotyped and analyzed for CNV determination by the Microarray Center at the Coriell Institute for Medical Research.

Karyotyping was performed on all cell cultures in the Repository with reported chromosome abnormalities. The samples chosen for genotyping in this study were intended to represent a diverse set of copy number variants, but the selection was also weighted to over-sample commonly manifested types of aberrations. When available, the ISCN description of the sample based on G-banding and FISH analysis is included in the phenotypic data. Karyotypes for these cells can be viewed in the online Repository catalog (http://ccr.coriell.org/Sections/Collections/NIGMS/?SsId=8).

Some field definitions from within an item description:

  • CN State: ...
  • Type: ...

The track displays CNVs labeled with type as Gain in black [this should be blue - to conform to convention in OMIM, ISCA and DECIPHER], and those with type Loss in light gray[this should be red]. The score field also corresponds to the type: all Gain CNVs have a score of 1000, and Loss CNVs have a score of 0.
